06 · Footprint, intuition, network & business

low-intensity · ~3–5 h/wk · off-clock (evenings, commutes). Four outputs, not a reading list — each pillar is something you build; reading is demoted to one load-bearing book apiece (three total). The slow-burn multiplier behind the technical lanes.

1 · Public technical writingthe footprint & letter-legibility channel — one engine, publish once everywhere
Ship a short public explainer on something you genuinely just learned — a GARCH/GAS or EVT result, a backtest you ran, an idea made concrete. The day it ships, it lands on your site + LinkedIn under one Zenodo DOI. This is the footprint — not blogging for its own sake — and it absorbs the old “identity” item entirely: publish once, everywhere, DOI'd. The footprint starts ageing from ~20, and that early date is itself the asset. Your teardowns (pillar 2) are the natural feedstock.
When: ORCID + the publish pipeline set up this summer (~1 evening) → ~1 piece every 6–8 weeks from autumn 2026, DOI'd + syndicated in the same action → Google Scholar waits for a real output.
One read: Williams & Bizup, Style: Lessons in Clarity and Grace — do the exercises.
2 · Risk-model failure teardownsintuition, earned as a validator — not as a reader
A private file of real blowups, each worked the way a model-validator would, not read about: LTCM '98, the 2008 Gaussian-copula machinery, the Aug-2007 quant quake, the 2022 UK LDI/gilt spiral, SVB duration, Archegos. Per case: what the risk model assumed → which assumption broke (fat tails, correlation → 1, funding/liquidity) → what an ES estimate or a backtest would have caught. This rehearses the exact reasoning of the job you're aiming at — the crisis canon becomes a teardown target, not a reading list.
When: 1 teardown per quarter, ongoing from summer 2026.
One read: Mandelbrot & Hudson, The (Mis)behavior of Markets — the tails book.
3 · Relationshipsa lean reflex, not a CRM program
Keep the ties you already have warm — the RESNA-win faculty & collaborators (the team that beat Cornell), the Kozha co-founders/backers, your DS&BA faculty — one line of record each and a zero-ask re-contact so none goes cold before you need letters and intros in 2027. No CRM theatre, no 48-hour rule, no seminar-question quota. The PI cold-open via the reproduction artifact is a 2027 move — it does not live here as a task yet.
When: ongoing reflex — touch each real tie ~2×/year.
4 · Boutique market teardownbusiness literacy, slow-burn — the most deferrable thing here
A slow private file reverse-engineering the regulatory-quant consulting market you actually aim at — real model-validation/risk boutiques (swissQuant and peers): who they bill, what a FINMA/Basel validation engagement is worth, the demand a single rule-change (FRTB, IRRBB) manufactures. Understand the business before you can ever price it.
When: ambient — revisit ~2×/year through 2027–28. Not an active task now.
One read (late): Maister, Managing the Professional Service Firm.
Off-clock, no schedule: the pop-risk canon (Kahneman, Taleb, Bernstein) and the news flow are leisure only — no line, no target. Probability lives in lane 02, Basel/FRTB primary docs in lane 03 — neither is repeated here.
End state (mid-2028): a DOI'd public-writing trail aged ~2 years, a bank of risk-model-failure teardowns that are the validator's reasoning, a handful of warm ties ready to write letters, and a real read on the boutique market — four outputs, three books, zero filler.
