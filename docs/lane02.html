02 · Math rigour

serious secondary · ~4–5 h/wk.

What this lane is. The mathematical foundation that makes the master’s hardest course easy. The German MSc has a compulsory, notoriously difficult measure-theoretic probability course (the rigorous foundation of probability). Arrive already fluent in it and that course becomes a review while classmates struggle — which frees the time for research. That is the whole flywheel.

The path and texts, in order. Real analysis first (the rigorous theory of limits, continuity, and integration on the real numbers), then measure-theoretic probability, then asymptotics (the theory of “as the sample grows large” — the law of large numbers, the central limit theorem — which underpins every estimator). Texts: Eccles (a gentle proof on-ramp) → Abbott, Understanding Analysis (the standard first course) → selective Pugh (harder, for depth) → Rosenthal / Williams (measure-theoretic probability).

It feeds, it does not gate. This is not required to start lane 03 — a GARCH estimator can be hand-coded without measure theory. It runs in parallel and pays off at the MSc. STOP-HERE: no topology, functional analysis, or stochastic calculus / Itô / SDEs — that is a different (pure-maths / derivatives-pricing) lane and a time sink; do not try to out-math the mathematicians. The graded external MATH 447 is dropped; this is ungraded self-study.

These are chapter-blocks of a hard subject, not weekly tasks — each season is a genuine ~4–5 h/week of proofs and problem sets.
