Roadmap — your multi-year map

The whole arc on one page: where you are now (Jun 2026) → a funded PhD. Read top-to-bottom when you lose the thread. Day-to-day to-dos live on the Tasks tab.

1. Where you're going

A funded research PhD → a high-comp job in a strong economy. Comp is the goal; the PhD is the vehicle, and PR is just what lets you take the job (US / EU / Canada all fine).

Which PhD, ranked: US top-20 ≥ strong UK/Swiss (Oxbridge · ETH/EPFL) ≥ {US top-40 ≈ Canadian}. Germany is only a last-line "never-stranded" floor. Apex US top-5 is a free swing, not the target — you won't burn years chasing it once a top-20 offer is in hand.

How you get in: apply direct + wide to US PhDs, and in parallel run a funded Canadian thesis-MSc as the bridge (Europe/Canada want a master's first, so it's infrastructure — and it feeds US/UK/Swiss/Canadian PhDs). Take the best funded offer when it comes. Most likely outcome: a funded sub-apex PhD — which already gets you the job and a place to stay.

5. The two gates that decide it

Everything above serves two things: (1) the MATH 447 A-grade (the real-analysis transcript line, Jun 2027) and (2) one calibrated research letter — so start the Lytkin/Mynbaev supervised project now, because it has to be mature by the Dec-2027 applications. Publications are not required. Protect these two and the rest is just execution.

6. When to stop

Accept and stop applying the moment a funded PhD you'd be happy in lands — US top-20, a strong UK/Swiss programme, or a good Canadian one. Apex top-5 is a bonus if it happens, not something to re-roll years for. A real offer in hand beats another cycle of waiting.

8. Money

PhD-primary. Income work is capped at ~10–15 h/wk (freelance / intern-app prep), just to defray the ~$60–90K MSc cost if the bridge is needed. Keep the Kozha funding paperwork going (~12 h/mo, Fri block) — but don't expand it into startup grinding.
