Higher-register reading habit (GRE vocab background, Jun–Oct 2026) — REVISED 2026-06

3 sessions/week, 30–40 min each. Slots into the lighter 19:00–22:00 evening block. Core principle: GRE Verbal tests the formal, literate, generalist vocabulary of the educated essayist (laconic, trenchant, obdurate, salutary, perfunctory) — NOT journalism (too plain) and NOT technical writing (jargon sits beside the GRE band, not above it). Target erudite long-form essays + review-essays.

Day Source What
MonLRB or NYRB or Harper's or TLS (rotate)One 3,000–6,000-word review-essay. Densest GRE-register prose available. Vocab + critical-reading modeling + endurance in a single piece.
WedLiterary-essay tradition (book)30-40 min slot from one of: Phillip Lopate, The Art of the Personal Essay (top single anthology) · Virginia Woolf, The Common Reader · Hazlitt / Lamb / De Quincey · Hitchens, Gore Vidal, Sontag, Trilling, Edmund Wilson · Samuel Johnson (densest end). One book at a time, slowly.
SatScience essayists (for GRE science RC prep)One longer piece (~5,000–10,000 words). Top pick: Stephen Jay Gould (real biology in erudite, allusive register) · Lewis Thomas · Loren Eiseley · Oliver Sacks. Current material: Quanta Magazine, Scientific American.

Reading mode: active, with vocabulary notebook open. Word you don't immediately know → jot down + definition + the sentence you saw it in. Context-anchored vocab sticks ~10× better than isolated flashcards (per your prior Anki experience).

What NOT to use: NYT · Atlantic · Economist · WSJ · mainstream journalism (register too plain — "hard words are easily understood from context, where they appear at all"). Aeon DOWNGRADED 2026-06: substantive ideas but moderate register (edited for broad global audience) — wrong register, not bad writing; ok occasional, not core. Research papers / HBR / business writing: technical jargon ≠ GRE words; skip.

Pair with spaced repetition: reading alone is a slow, uneven way to cover the GRE word list in 9 months. Reading gives depth + contextual feel; GregMat (free Vocab Mountain + YouTube during background phase) gives coverage. The two together converge faster than either alone.
