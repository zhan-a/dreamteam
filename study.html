4. Classical causal inference textbooks REFERENCE — 2027+

REFERENCE shelf — not active summer 2026 reading. Hernán-Robins Ch 1-4 + 12 will be pulled when Phase 1 bends toward conformal-causal (post-MATH 447, 2027). Until then these are background texts to consult if needed, not a reading queue.

  • Hernán & Robins 2024 — Causal Inference: What If. Free at hsph.harvard.edu/miguel-hernan/causal-inference-book/. Modern standard for biostat causal inference. Parts I (Ch 1-10) + II (Ch 11-17) + III (Ch 18-25). Cite explicitly in SoP.
  • Imbens & Rubin 2015 — Causal Inference for Statistics, Social, and Biomedical Sciences: An Introduction. Cambridge. Potential-outcomes framework canonical text.
  • Pearl 2009 — Causality: Models, Reasoning, and Inference. 2nd ed. Cambridge. The do-calculus reference.
  • Pearl, Glymour & Jewell 2016 — Causal Inference in Statistics: A Primer. Wiley. Light entry to Pearl's framework; ~150 pages.
  • VanderWeele 2015 — Explanation in Causal Inference: Methods for Mediation and Interaction. Oxford. Standard mediation analysis reference.
  • Rosenbaum 2002, 2010 — Observational Studies (2002) and Design of Observational Studies (2010). Matching + sensitivity analysis canonical.
  • Robins, Hernán & Brumback 2000 — "Marginal structural models and causal inference in epidemiology". Epidemiology 11(5):550–560. The MSM founding paper.

5. Real analysis + math foundations textbooks ABBOTT + A&H + RUDIN ACTIVE

Lever 1 self-study path (Summer 2026 → MATH 447 Feb 2027): Abbott (bridge) → Rudin (UIUC coursework). For MT1173 algebra prep (Nov 2026 exam): Anthony & Harvey. Pugh and Tao are accessible alternatives to Rudin if stuck. Davidson & Donsig is the Athabasca MATH 370 textbook (Athabasca DEPRIORITIZED — accreditation lost Mar 2025). Russian / Soviet rigorous texts (Zorich, Fichtenholz, Kolmogorov-Fomin, Natanson) apply if you enroll at NU or KazNU.

  • Abbott 2015 — Understanding Analysis, 2nd ed. Springer. The Lever 1 summer 2026 self-study text. Ch 1-8 by end Aug 2026 is the floor. Gentler bridge into Rudin: same content arc with more proofs spelled out and exercises that build intuition before rigor. Each chapter teaches WHY the careful definitions matter via a "discussion" → "main results" → "epilogue" structure. Pen-and-paper proofs are the practice mode.
  • Anthony & Harvey 2012 — Linear Algebra: Concepts and Methods. Cambridge. The UoL MT1173 Algebra primary text (Nov 2026 exam). Ch 1-12 cover matrices, vector spaces, linear maps, eigenvalues, inner products. UoL past papers are the second half of prep. Prep starts mid-Jul 2026 at 15-25 hr/wk, ramps to 30-35 hr/wk by Oct.
  • Rudin 1976 — Principles of Mathematical Analysis, 3rd ed. McGraw-Hill. "Baby Rudin." Ch 1-7 are the Math Gate target — UIUC NetMath MATH 447 (Feb-Jun 2027) covers Ch 1-7; A-grade is the gate for the apex tail. Ch 11 is Lebesgue intro (not needed for MATH 447). Name explicitly in SoP. Self-study Ch 1-4 in fall 2026 before MATH 447 starts.
  • Axler 2024 — Linear Algebra Done Right, 4th ed. Springer (free PDF). Lever 1b — proof-based linear algebra self-study, Sep 2026 onward. MT1173 covers computational LA (matrices, Cramer's rule, eigenvalues operationally) but NOT proof-based finite-dim vector spaces / linear maps / spectral theorem — apex Stats committees + Cambridge MASt MASS expect this at entry. Axler's anti-determinant approach forces structural thinking. Target: Ch 1-3 (vector spaces, finite-dim, linear maps) light parallel Sep-Dec 2026; Ch 4-5 (polynomials, eigenvalues) Jan-Jun 2027 alongside MATH 447; Ch 6-8 (inner product spaces, operators) Summer 2027 if bandwidth. Discipline: apply the 45-min no-premature-consultation rule + cold-reproduction protocol per Schedule "Self-study discipline" card. Self-study replaces formal UIUC MATH 416 ($1,653 saved) per 2026-06-05 cost-benefit decision; for SOP credibility, build a public artifact (GitHub repo with chapter writeups OR Stack Exchange profile answering LA questions). Mention with specificity in SOP: "Self-studied Axler Ch 1-5; can articulate finite-dim spectral theorem and its applications to PCA." Marginal-but-non-trivial signal lift; ~70-80% of MATH 416 signal value at $0 cost IF discipline rule actually applied.
  • Friedberg, Insel & Spence 2018 — Linear Algebra, 5th ed. Pearson. Slightly gentler alternative to Axler with more worked examples; equally accepted as proof-based LA reference. Use as second-opinion if Axler proofs feel too compressed.
  • Royden 2010 — Real Analysis, 4th ed. (with Fitzpatrick). Lebesgue measure + integration; Ch 1-5 is the measure-theoretic target. PhD Y1 reference, NOT pre-PhD.
  • Pugh 2015 — Real Mathematical Analysis, 2nd ed. Springer. More expository than Rudin; same content. Pugh + Rudin together is the standard pairing — use as second-opinion when Rudin's compression bites.
  • Tao 2016 — Analysis I + Analysis II, 4th ed. Springer / Hindustan Book Agency. Free PDFs on Tao's blog. Constructive build-up from naturals. Use as third-opinion alternative to Rudin.
  • Bartle & Sherbert 2011 — Introduction to Real Analysis, 4th ed. Wiley. UIUC NetMath MATH 444 textbook (NOT 447 — 444 is the calc-style intro, 447 uses Rudin).
  • Davidson & Donsig 2010 — Real Analysis and Applications: Theory in Practice. Springer. Athabasca MATH 370 textbook. Proof-based; covers Rudin Ch 1-7 equivalent. Athabasca deprioritized — MSCHE accreditation lost Mar 2025.
  • Zorich — Mathematical Analysis Vol I + II. Springer. Russian/Soviet rigorous standard (used at MGU mech-mat). If KazNU offers this textbook → strong signal.
  • Fichtenholz — Курс дифференциального и интегрального исчисления, 3 vols. Russian classic. Used at top Russian math departments.
  • Kolmogorov & Fomin — Элементы теории функций и функционального анализа. Russian classic for measure theory + functional analysis (= Royden + Folland equivalent).
  • Natanson — Theory of Functions of a Real Variable. Russian measure-theoretic reference.
